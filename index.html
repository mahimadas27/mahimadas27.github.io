About Me

I'm Mahima Das, an academic researcher passionate about the intersection of technology, humanities, and creative expression. My work explores how digital tools can enhance research methodologies and knowledge dissemination in the humanities and social sciences.

With a background in both technical and humanistic disciplines, I approach research questions with a unique perspective that bridges quantitative analysis and qualitative interpretation. I'm particularly interested in how visualization techniques can make complex research findings more accessible and engaging.

Currently investigating: How interactive data visualizations can enhance the comprehension and engagement with qualitative research findings in academic publications.

Outside of research, I'm an avid reader, occasional writer, and music enthusiast who believes that diverse interests fuel creative academic work. I maintain several open-source projects aimed at making research tools more accessible to academics across disciplines.
